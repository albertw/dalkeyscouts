What Scouters (Leaders) Do

📅 Plan Activities

Work with other leaders to plan weekly meetings, camps, and special activities that are fun, safe, and help young people learn and grow.

👥 Lead Meetings

Run weekly meetings, guiding young people through games, activities, and badge work. You'll work as part of a team, so you're never on your own.

🏕️ Attend Camps

Help supervise and run weekend camps and summer expeditions. Camping with young people is incredibly rewarding and lots of fun!

🤝 Build Relationships

Mentor and support young people, helping them develop confidence, skills, and friendships. Be a positive role model they can look up to.

📚 Learn New Skills

Develop leadership, communication, and organizational skills through Scouting Ireland's comprehensive training program.

😄 Have Fun!

Scouting isn't just for young people — our leaders have great fun too! You'll make new friends and have adventures.

Requirements

To become a scout leader, you need to:

1

Be over 18 years old

2

Complete Scouting Ireland's vetting process (Garda vetting, references)

3

Attend foundation training within your first few months

4

Be willing to commit to regular attendance (we understand work/life balance!)

5

Have enthusiasm for working with young people

6

Be willing to learn and develop new skills

No previous scouting experience is necessary! Many of our best leaders had no scouting background before joining. We provide full training and support.
👥

Types of Volunteering Roles

👨‍🏫

Scouter

Work directly with young people, planning and delivering weekly meetings and activities. You'll be part of a leadership team for one of our sections: Beavers, Cubs, or Scouts.

📊

Group Council Member

Support the group behind the scenes through administration, finance, fundraising, facilities management, or other essential roles that keep the group running.

🎯

Skills Instructor

Share your expertise! If you have skills in outdoor activities, water sports, first aid, or other areas, we'd love to have you as an occasional instructor.

⭐

Ensure Your Child's Place

Volunteering as a leader helps ensure your child has a place in our group. With our sections currently at capacity, becoming a leader is one of the best ways to guarantee your child can participate in scouting.
